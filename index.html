The Four Laws

Rules of Existence

01
Underpopulation

Fewer than 2 neighbors?
Cell dies.

A live cell with 0 or 1 live neighbours dies, as if by loneliness. Life needs community to survive.

02
Survival

2 or 3 neighbors?
Cell lives on.

A live cell with 2 or 3 live neighbours survives to the next generation. Balance sustains life.

03
Overpopulation

More than 3 neighbors?
Cell dies.

A live cell with more than 3 live neighbours dies, as if by overcrowding. Even abundance can kill.

04
Reproduction

Exactly 3 neighbors?
New life emerges.

A dead cell with exactly 3 live neighbours becomes alive. Creation from the right conditions.

Getting Started

How to Play

STEP 01

Draw Your Pattern

Click on any cell in the grid to toggle it alive or dead. Design your initial configuration — this is your seed.

STEP 02

Start the Simulation

Press Play to begin. Watch as each generation unfolds according to the four rules, creating emergent complexity.

STEP 03

Observe & Iterate

Pause, rewind, adjust speed. Experiment with different patterns. Discover gliders, oscillators, and still lifes.
